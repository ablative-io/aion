# AD-009: Recovery on startup

> **Cluster:** aion-durability
> **Depends on:** AD-008, AD-002
> **Checklist:**
> - C34 — recover calls EventStore::list_active and, for each returned WorkflowId, reads its history and replays it to its resume point.
> - C35 — recover initialises each workflow's Recorder at the head implied by its existing history so post-recovery appends continue at the correct sequence.
> - C36 — recover surfaces a per-workflow failure (e.g. a NonDeterminismError during replay) without silently dropping the workflow or aborting recovery of the others.
> - C37 — A test seeds InMemoryStore with multiple active and terminal workflows and asserts recover replays exactly the active ones to their correct resume points.
> **Stories:**
> - S6 (Operator, Recovering and Diagnosing) — As an operator, I want every active workflow to be replayed and resumed automatically on engine startup so that no in-flight workflow is silently lost after a restart.
> - S8 (Operator, Recovering and Diagnosing) — As an operator, I want a failure recovering one workflow not to abort recovery of the others so that one bad workflow cannot take down the whole engine restart.

## Purpose

Make crash recovery complete and automatic. On engine startup, every non-terminal workflow must be replayed from its full history and resumed at its resume point, with each workflow's Recorder initialised at the correct head so post-recovery appends continue cleanly. A failure recovering one workflow (e.g. a non-determinism violation) must be reported without silently dropping that workflow or aborting recovery of the others.

## Task

Implement the recovery routine (recovery.rs). It calls EventStore::list_active and, for each WorkflowId, reads its history, builds a Recorder via resume_at at the head implied by that history, and replays the workflow to its resume point (delegating to AD-008's replay). It collects per-workflow outcomes — recovered-to-resume-point, recovered-to-terminal, or failed — so one bad workflow cannot abort the rest. Test by seeding InMemoryStore with multiple active and terminal workflows (including one with a divergent history) and asserting recover handles exactly the active ones correctly and reports the failure for the bad one.

## Requirements

### R1: List-active and per-workflow replay

WHEN recover is invoked, THE SYSTEM SHALL call EventStore::list_active and, for each returned WorkflowId, read its history and replay it to its resume point.

**Acceptance:**
- recovery.rs defines recover taking the store and LiveExecutor handles.
- It calls list_active, then for each id calls read_history and drives AD-008 replay to the resume point.
- Terminal workflows are not returned by list_active (per AC contract) and so are not replayed; recover does not re-derive activeness itself.
- A unit test with a store stub returning two active ids asserts both are read and replayed.

### R2: Recorder initialised at the existing head

WHILE recovering a workflow, THE SYSTEM SHALL initialise its Recorder at the head implied by its existing recorded history (via Recorder::resume_at) so that appends after recovery continue at the correct sequence.

**Acceptance:**
- For a workflow with K recorded events, recover constructs its Recorder at head K.
- A test recovers a workflow with K events, then drives one live step post-recovery and asserts the new event lands at sequence K (no SequenceConflict).
- The head is derived from the read history length/last sequence, not guessed.

### R3: Per-workflow failure isolation

IF replaying one workflow during recovery fails (e.g. a NonDeterminismError or a HistoryShape error), THEN THE SYSTEM SHALL record/report that workflow's failure and SHALL continue recovering the remaining workflows; it SHALL NOT abort the whole recovery or silently drop the failed workflow.

**Acceptance:**
- recover returns a per-workflow outcome report (e.g. Vec of {WorkflowId, RecoveryOutcome}) where RecoveryOutcome is Resumed{resume_point} | Terminal | Failed(DurabilityError).
- A workflow that diverges on replay yields Failed(NonDeterminismError) in the report and the workflow is failed deterministically per AD-005 (one WorkflowFailed recorded), while other workflows still recover.
- A test seeds three workflows — two clean active, one divergent — and asserts the report contains two Resumed and one Failed, and that the two clean ones reached their resume points.
- recover itself returns Ok with the report for partial-failure cases; it returns Err only for a store-level failure that prevents listing at all.

## Boundaries

- SHALL NOT spawn the BEAM workflow processes or wire into the engine's supervision tree — AE invokes recover and owns process creation.
- SHALL NOT re-implement active-vs-terminal classification — it trusts EventStore::list_active (AC contract).
- SHALL NOT execute live timers/signals during recovery beyond the resume-point handoff — live behaviour is AT/AE post-resume.
